About Us

Tracy Anderson has owned and operated the successful business since March 2012. Tracy has been joined by other like minded Podiatrists who work as Associates.

Podiatrists are highly skilled health professionals who have been trained to BSc Honours Degree level to diagnose, treat, and provide specialist advice on how to prevent and rehabilitate abnormal conditions of the feet and lower limbs.

They also aim to keep people mobile and active, relieve pain and treat infections. Some Podiatric problems are inherited; some develop from the negative effects of systemic illnesses, from the pressure of ill-fitting shoes or physical trauma.

Our Podiatrists

Tracy Anderson BSc (Hons) PGCert MRCPod

Since 1997 Tracy has practiced in both NHS and Private Practice and has achieved the following qualifications:

  • BSc Hons Podiatry (Queen Margaret University, Edinburgh)
  • Dip Diabetes in Primary Care (Bradford University)
  • PGCert Sports Podiatry (Manchester Metropolitan University)
  • BTEC Swedish Massage (Jewel and Esk Valley College, Edinburgh)

Tracy thrives in the Clinic environment. She has a special interest in developing relationships with patients to influence how they manage their own foot care and medical conditions that affect their feet. Keeping patients active is what Tracy is all about. This may involve adapting shoes or advising on training routines, removing corns, proving padding or strapping or prescribing orthotics.


Joanna Allan BSc (Hons) MRCPod

Joanna qualified from Queen Margaret University in 2013, has worked at Melrose Foot Care as an Associate Podiatrist since June 2015 and covers all aspects of Podiatry care including nail care, corns, verrucae, ulcerations, musculoskeletal assessments and Orthotics, She has a particular niche in treating Ingrown Toenails. This covers education on self care, conservative treatment and nail removal surgery under local anaesthetic if required. She thoroughly enjoys her work and takes great pride in providing care to the best of her ability.


Caroline Fairbairn BSc (Hons) MRCPod

Caroline studied at Queen Margaret University and qualified as a Podiatrist in 2015. She has previous experience of working within the NHS and Private Practice. At present Caroline is solely working within the private setting and enjoys working as part of a team. She is enthusiastic about all aspects of Podiatry, with an understanding of foot pain and discomfort, and the impact it can have on a person’s quality of life. Areas of interest are the management of ingrown toenails and 'at risk' foot conditions, such as diabetes. Caroline has a passion for promoting excellent foot health, and it is important to her that patients feel comfortable and at ease while being treated.
